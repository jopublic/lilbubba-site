K8 Is Live: How One Person Built a Software Company From Scratch

By Jo Browning · 27 March 2026 · 5 min read

Today, K8 officially went live. You can go to lilbuba.ai, click "Buy Now," pay for a licence, download the installer, and start finding duplicate files on your machine. The whole thing takes about two minutes.

Getting to those two minutes took three months.

The Problem That Started It All

I'm a musician, filmmaker, and DJ. Which means I have an absolutely ridiculous number of files. Photos in five different folders. Videos in three different formats. Music tracks exported, re-exported, backed up, synced across devices, and scattered across drives I'd forgotten existed.

When I ran out of space on my laptop for the fourth time in a month, I finally snapped. I downloaded every duplicate finder I could find. They all did the same thing: hash every file on the drive, compare every hash, and take hours to finish. On my 500GB drive, one tool estimated 6 hours. I fell asleep waiting.

There had to be a better way.

The 4am Breakthrough

I do my best thinking at stupid o'clock. Always have. There's something about 4am when the world's quiet, the tea's strong, and nobody's asking you for anything.

The insight was simple: you don't need to hash every file. Most files on your computer are obviously unique - different sizes, different types, different dates. You can eliminate 99% of candidates before you even start comparing content. The hashing is only needed for the final 1%.

That one idea turned a 6-hour scan into a 6-second scan.

Building With AI (The Honest Version)

I'm going to say something that might surprise you: I built K8 with AI assistants. Not as a gimmick. Not as a marketing angle. Because I'm one person with a big idea and no development team.

Here's what AI actually did: it wrote code when I told it what to build. It debugged things I couldn't see. It designed the installer. It helped me file a patent. It built the website you're reading right now.

Here's what AI didn't do: come up with the idea, understand the problem, make the design decisions, test it on real messy data, or care whether it actually worked well for real people. That was all me, at 4am, with a cup of tea and a very patient dog.

"We took the intelligence out of AI so you don't have to."

That's the LilBuba.ai tagline, and it's not just marketing. K8 runs 100% on your machine. No cloud uploads. No accounts. No subscriptions phoning home. The clever part is baked into the software - it does its job, then shuts up. Your files never leave your computer.

What K8 Actually Does

There are three tools in the K8 family:

  • K8 Bionic - finds duplicate files and similar photos. The one that started it all.
  • K8 Cruft - finds developer junk. node_modules, __pycache__, build artifacts, caches. Scores everything by safety so you know what's genuinely safe to delete. There's literally nothing else on the market that does this properly.
  • K8 Media - for creators drowning in footage. Finds same-video-different-encode matches, pairs RAW files with JPEG exports, and respects your editor project folders.

Everything goes through a 30-day quarantine before permanent deletion. Nothing is truly gone until you're absolutely sure.
